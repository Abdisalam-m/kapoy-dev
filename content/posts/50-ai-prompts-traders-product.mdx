I spent a few evenings curating 50 prompt templates for traders using Claude and GPT-4, packaged them into a PDF, and sold it for $29 on Gumroad. Made about $2k in the first month, mostly passive. Here's what I learned.

## The idea

Trading is repetitive: "give me a market summary," "analyze this chart," "what are the risks?" — the same queries over and over. I thought, what if I just pre-wrote 50 solid prompts that traders could copy/paste into Claude or ChatGPT and get immediate answers?

Each prompt covered:
- Market analysis (macro, micro)
- Risk assessment
- Position sizing
- Journaling templates (for post-trade review)
- Emotional resilience checks ("am I about to panic sell?")

50 seemed like a number — enough to feel comprehensive, not so many that it felt bloated.

## Building it

I used a Google Sheet to organize:

| Prompt Name        | Category      | Use Case                       | Prompt Text                |
| ------------------ | ------------- | ------------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| Macro Summary      | Analysis      | Get a quick macro picture      | "Give me a 3-minute macro..." |
| Risk Per Trade     | Position Size | Calculate safe position size   | "I have a $50k account..." |
| Trade Journal      | Post-Trade    | Review what just happened      | "I entered at X, exited at Y..." |

Then I:
1. Tested each one against both Claude and ChatGPT
2. Tweaked them based on what returned the most useful output
3. Organized them in a Figma template with consistent formatting
4. Exported to PDF (used Figma → PDF, much cleaner than Word)

The whole thing took about 20 hours spread over two weeks.

## Selling it

I uploaded it to Gumroad, set the price at $29, and posted it in:
- Twitter (my followers from the trading bot posts)
- A few trading Discord communities (the nice ones; not spammy)
- My Substack newsletter (50 subscribers at the time)

Gumroad handles everything: payments (via Stripe), delivery, affiliate tracking. I literally just uploaded the PDF and left it.

```
Week 1: 12 sales = $348
Week 2: 18 sales = $522
Week 3: 8 sales = $232
Week 4: 5 sales = $145
```

After month one, it dropped to ~2-3 sales per week. But it's been steady enough that I check Gumroad once a week out of habit.

## The margins are absurd

Gumroad takes 10% + payment processing (~2.2%). So per $29 sale, I net ~$25.30. No hosting costs, no customer support (it's a PDF), no updates required unless I want to.

This is why digital products are addictive for indie makers — the unit economics are insane compared to services.

```
$25.30 per sale × 80 sales (month 1) = ~$2,024
Effort: ~25 hours total (including marketing) = $81/hour

Compare to:
Service work: $50-100/hour, all your time, scaling is slow
```

## What I'd do differently

**Better positioning** — I marketed it as "for any trader," but it probably performs better for beginners or people new to using AI for trading. Should've leaned into that.

**Bundle it** — I could've paired the PDF with a 30-min Loom video of me *using* the prompts in real-time with Claude. Would've justified $49-59 and probably increased conversions.

**Evergreen funnel** — The initial Twitter/Discord push carried most of the sales. I should've built an email funnel or written a blog post about AI prompts for traders that ranks in search.

**Upsell** — Sell the PDF at $19, then pitch a $99 "prompt engineering workshop" or a Zapier automation kit that feeds trading alerts into Claude automatically. Layer the offers.

## The real lesson

The product itself wasn't special — any trader could've done the same. The real asset was *distribution*. Those 50 Twitter followers, the Discord relationships, the Substack — that's what moved units.

If you're thinking about a digital product, don't overthink it. Pick something you know well, spend a week on it, and ship it. The real value is in the audience you bring to it, not the product itself.

And Gumroad makes it stupid simple — literally no reason not to try.
